This section traces the four key flows: hold a seat, contention rejection, confirm payment, and hold expiry release.

This section explains the seat state machine: available → held (with TTL) → booked, and why holds must expire to prevent seat leakage.

<h2 id="concurrency-control">Concurrency Control</h2>

This section is the centerpiece: the oversell race condition, optimistic concurrency (compare-and-set / conditional UPDATE WHERE status='available'), and pessimistic locking (SELECT FOR UPDATE).

<h2 id="strong-consistency">Strong Consistency</h2>

This section explains why the inventory of record must be strongly consistent, what "strongly consistent" means in this context, and the tradeoffs with eventual consistency approaches.

This section covers the idempotent hold-pay-confirm saga: charging payment atomically, flipping held→booked, and handling partial failures with idempotency keys.
